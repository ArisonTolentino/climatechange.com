Topic: Climate Change and Global Warming

Around at the middle of the 1800s and during the industrial revolution was
the start of a global crisis. Physicist John Tyndall recognized Earth's natural
greenhouse effect and suggested that slight changes in the atmospheric composition
could bring about climatic variations. In 1896, a Swedish scientist Svante Arrhenius was
the first to predict that changes in the atmospheric carbon dioxide levels could substantially
alter the surface temperature through the greenhouse effect. By the late 1950s, CO2 readings would
give some of the first data to confirm the global warming theory. The UN says that our world is hotter
by 1C since pre-industrial times and it is okay, in fact the UN says if we reach 1.5C by the end of the
century we would be fine and even 2C, but CO2 level keeps rising faster and faster and this could mean catastrophe
since the temperature of Earth is about to reach 1.5C in only just 10 years.



Climate change is not only hurting us but it is also hurting animals, plants,
and Earth. Human activities is the cause of the crisis and as we keep burning fossil
fuels, coals, oils, and gasses, it will produce more CO2 therefore climate change and
global warming will keep getting worse. Our planet keeps getting warmer because the greenhouse
gasses in the atmosphere prevent some of the heat from returning directly to space, this is
the Greenhouse effect. Deforastation is happening since trees are being burned down, sea levels are
rising because of the icebergs melting. Climate change can be slowed down if we work together and act together.
We can slow it down by reducing the use of cars and instead use bikes or walk. We need more people to be informed
about this so we could potentially save our world and have a future to look forward into.
